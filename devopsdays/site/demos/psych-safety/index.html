      The takeaway

      Blame optimizes for hiding. Blamelessness optimizes for fixing.

      In 2026, humans and agents both ship fast — which means mistakes arrive fast too. The only way to learn fast enough to keep up is psychological safety: people, and the logs of what the agents did, must surface problems the instant they appear, with zero fear. The moment someone gets blamed, everyone else learns to hide — disclosure slows, MTTR climbs, and the same incident comes back wearing a different name. Safe teams aren't soft. They're faster, because nothing stays hidden long enough to hurt. You fix systems, not people.
